BIO

I was born in 1964 in the USSR. Studied at the school, worked at a factory, served in the army, studied at the institute. And then this country came to an end. It happened. From 1991 to May 2021, I lived in Russia. In May 2021, I left the country for good. That's what I thought when I left. So I think now. Living in the country has become completely impossible. On February 24, 2022, Putin approved my decision to emigrate.

You have already read this once on qrz. I'll add details.

Childhood was happy and carefree. Father was a submarine officer. As they said then - the nuclear shield of the country. I grew up in Kamchatka (RAØZ land). There he took up the soldering iron for the first time. Assembled a simple detector receiver.

In 1978, my father retired. For the last three years of school, I studied in the North Caucasus. First sex, first love, first (and last) drug... Everything was there. Then I worked in a factory and studied at radio school before the army. To become a radio operator. That's where I got into amateur radio.

Before receiving the first call sign, younsgters gained experience at the collective (club) station of the radio school in the city of Nalchik UK6XAA. Where I was an errand boy - to hold the antenna being fixed so it doesn’t fall and run for vodka for the "right kind" of hams.

From 1982 to 1984 I served in the army for two years. I promised not to tell anyone about this. I even signed a paper. 40 years have passed and the country I promised to keep my mouth shut is not there no more. But once you give your word, you have to keep it.

Then the coup in August 1991. The death of the country in which I grew up. New Russia. A year of turmoil in the White House with radio station R3A.

Then I was kicked out and from the autumn of 1992 I just lived a normal life.

Was married for 10 years. I was happy. I went along with the country through all its stages of its transformation, as it grew into today's monster.

And two years ago, my patience have ran out. Things have gotten worse. I could not stand the transformation of the country into Gulag and decided to leave.

If not wasn't for the stroke I had in 2016 maybe I would have stayed. To fight for a different tomorrow. But with poor health, what kind of a fighter am I? (A little pathos).

This is how I left Russia in May 2021.

However, Putin continues to affect my mindset. I wish to see him gone from the face of the Earth.
